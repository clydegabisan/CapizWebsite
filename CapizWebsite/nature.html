NATURE

Baybay Beach

Baybay, Roxas City, Capiz

Baybay Beach is the prime destination of Roxas City. Located 3 kilometers away from the city proper, this 12-kilometer stretch of black sand is a popular weekend spot for the local populace and those coming from the nearby towns and provinces. Considered a “must-see” spot while in Roxas City, Baybay Beach is ideal for swimming, snorkeling, boating, kayaking, skim boarding and other beach and water sports.

Cadimahan River Tour

Lawis Baybay, Roxas City, Capiz

Opening out into Baybay Beach where Cadimahan River had its mouth is refuge to the marine life that survive only in contending fresh and seawater.  Shouldered on both sides by varieties of mangroves is also an abode for those who seek peace and relaxation.  Local folks offer an excitement of a dinner in a raft while serenade of folk songs makes tourist seem transported to paradise.  Traditional massage by trained “hilot” makes an experience complete.

PALINA RIVER TOUR

Cagay, Roxas City, Capiz

Located in Sitio Cablatan, Brgy. Cagay, Roxas City, Palina River and its adjacent areas are economic zones. The river bank lined with mangroves is about 1 km. in length not including the tributaries. Three thousand group cages are installed in the river with seafood they can buy and cook themselves or have them cooked by the fisherfolks trained in food preparation.  

Palina Greenbelt Eco-Park offers mangrove tour, river tour and beach swimming. The Roxas City River Tours began as a program by the City Coastal Resource and Management Office for the preservation of our mangroves and river systems. Floating cabanas used for rent and used for tours, come in different sizes with different holding capacity. Now, the ecopark has become an educational recreation facility and a venue for community participation in rehabilitating the river and the mangrove ecosystem.

In 2016, it was declared grand winner of DOT-ATOP Pearl Awards for Best Practices in Community-based Responsible Tourism Enterprise. Last January 26, 2018, the ecopark was given the Best Urban Sustainable Product Award during the ASEAN Sustainable Tourism Awards in Chiang Mai, Thailand.

Culajao Mangrove Ecopark

Culajao, Roxas City, Capiz

Located at Barangay. Culajao, Roxas City, the Culajao Mangrove Ecopark is best for educational tours, family outings and group fellowship. Nineteen (19) species of mangroves is found in the ecopark that also serve as a habitat for a wide variety of fishes, crustaceans, mollusks, birds and insects.

Hinulugan Falls

Tabun-acan, Pilar, Capiz

The well-known Hinulugan Falls of Barangay Tabun-acan is one of the tourist spots of the municipality of Pilar in Capiz. Travelers go on 30-minute hike to get to the top of the falls. There is a small chapel near the falls where mass can be heard. The area is also open to those who would like to conduct retreats by appointment. Hinulugan Falls serves as a water source of the community surrounding the falls and is considered as one of the major tourist attractions in the province of Capiz. According to tales passed from one generation to the next, there was a hunter who happened to chase after a deer in the forest. Not knowing that there was a waterfall ahead of him, he fell off the edge and into the waters. Thus, it was named Hinulugan.

BALISONG CAVE COMPLEX

Pilar, Capiz

Balisong Cave is also known as Butete Cave. It has beautiful rock formations made of limestones. Its karsts formation and wildlife are unique and its limestone is one of the oldest in Panay Island. While it is of archaeological and ecological importance, it’s also of historical significance as it once was the graveyard of Balisong heroes. Artificants were buried inside the cave. It was also declared as a bird sanctuary.

Liktinon Falls

Bayuyan, Pres. Roxas, Capiz

Liktinon falls is a natural-layered waterfall located within the protected forest areas of Barangay Bayuyan, President Roxas, Capiz. It has two known levels. The first level is 8 to 9 feet tall and is inclined or sloped. The swimming area spans 40 to 60 meters and has a depth of 13 to 16 feet. The water is cool and clear since the base of the area is filled with rocks. The surrounding forest area adds serenity and relaxation to those who swim. A few hiking steps above the first level is the second level which is taller at about 11 to 14 feet tall. Because of the difficulty in climbing, the second level is seldom visited. The trek going up to Liktinon falls is an adventure itself since you have to endure jumping from one boulder to another before reaching the falls and the swimming area.

The locals named the place to describe the process in going up to the waterfalls which is, “naga liktin-liktin” or to jump from one place to another hence, “Liktinon”.

The natural beauty and the challenging trails going up to the Liktinon Falls attract adventurous tourists. It is an eco-tourism site that is also a livelihood source for the people of Barangay Bayuyan.

Capiz Eco-Park and Cultural Village

Nagba, Cuartero, Capiz

Situated in Barangay Nagba in the Municipality of Cuartero, the Capiz Ecology Park nestles within a 14-hectare landmass of undulating terrain all covered with green vegetation comprised of trees, shrubs and bamboos, among others. Likewise, there are small farm lots devoted to rice production and forage plots as protein bank as well as ornamental gardens within the park’s landscape.

Capiz Eco-Park is the best place for those who seek a peaceful haven away from the city. It’s an ideal venue for family picnic, retreats, seminars, team-building activities, camps, and even just for anyone looking for a breather from a busy life. It also has a playground for kids and kids-at-heart who still enjoy playing outdoor games and running barefooted on the grass. 

Lahab Cave

San Miguel, Dumalag, Capiz

Fanciful formations of stalactites and stalagmites make this a fascinating underground world. Its side walls are solid strontium and the ceilings are arched with prismatic crystals that flash and twinkle. On its floor are terraced miniature dams of cool water. The scenic hills and peaceful valleys on the surface are part of the place’s attraction.

Basiao Beach

Basiao, Ivisan, Capiz

Only a thirty-minute ride from the town center, is the Basiao Beach, a long stretch of white sand in the coastal barangay of Basiao, Ivisan, Capiz, and the nearest jump-off point to the four nearby islands namely Marukol Dako, Marukol Gamay, Mahabang Pulo and Mabaay Island.

Lined-up along the highway are beach resorts offering cottages, bathing areas and rooms for tourists coming from the nearby municipalities and provinces.

Agustin Navarra Mangrove Forest and Bird Sanctuary

Agustin Navarra, Ivisan, Capiz

The Agustin Navarra Mangrove Forest and Bird Sanctuary is an emerging ecotourism attraction in the province. Located in Agustin Navarra, Ivisan, Capiz, the place is home to local and migratory birds such as the Dugwak (Little Regret), Kuru (Green Backed Heron), Tulabong (Great Egret), Mansong (Marsh Tern), Sterna and Lapay (Pond Heron).

The indigenous species of mangroves that grow in the area include Bakawan Lalaki/Bakawan Babae – Phizophora, Bungalon – Avecenia Marina, Api-Api – Officinales, Piapi – Lanaya, Bungalon Puti – Arba and mangroves of the family Sonneratiaceae pedada.

These mangroves are natural barriers for storm surges and high tide, and a habitat for these native and migratory birds that has grown in population since its forests’ rehabilitation.

Kipot Cave

Burias, Mambusao, Capiz

Kipot cave system is composed of at least 24 caves each with stream going through it. One of these caves is the Napandong Cave, a wet cave with a navigable stream going through it. Since this cave is inaccessible during floods, the cave formations are intact and well-preserved. The speleothems found at the cave ceilings, just above the water, are numerous, large, and active. A latent eco-tourism site, the cave is inhabited by different species of insects, bats and birds. A species of birds called “Salimbabatang” (swift birds) is a potential tourist attraction. The nests of the said bird are source of income for the locals. Speleothems or cave deposits such as stalactites and stalagmites are found inside the cave. Cave formations such as draperies and canopies are dominant. Other formations found are flowstones, columns, soda straws, canopies, bottlebrushes, coralloid/botroyoidal/ popcorn forms, shelf stone, and moon milk.

Suhoton Cave

Agcagay, Jamindan, Capiz

Suhoton Cave is made up of 3 stocks and 48 chambers. There is the presence of the stalactites and stalagmites. The animals present at suhoton cave are bats, monkeys, snakes, centipedes, crabs, turtles, snails and lizards. It was said to be a hiding place of the Filipino and American soldiers from the Japanese during World War II. In the center of the Suhoton Cave, there is a wide area called Baylihan where it was told that it was used by American soldiers as party area where they brought women and let them dance. One of the chambers of the Suhoton Cave is believed to be the way to get to the municipality of Dumalag. There is also a chamber in the cave with a rock that sounds as loud as an agong instrument once hit. It is also believed that in Suhoton Cave, there is a tower that offers a view of the whole area of Camp Peralta once you go through it.

Marugo Lake

San Antonio, Tapaz, Capiz

Marugo Lake is a 20-minute drive from the town proper of Tapaz to its humble barangay, San Antonio. It is considered as a prime trip destination in the municipality during summer break. Surrounded by a long range of green hills, Lake Marugo’s jade-hued waters reflect the blue sky and the hills – a natural sight good for tourists searching for a peaceful break.  

Lake Marugo also provides the best place for outdoor activities like swimming, boating, fishing, trekking, rafting and camping. A swimming pool on the elevated part of the lake’s bank offers an excellent viewpoint of the lake and the hills. Renting floating cottages in the middle of the lake is also a good option for the guests.  

According to the locals, during the old days, the fresh catch would drain blood from being caught, thus, “gadugo-dugo,” which was what the lake was named after.

Kataw Falls

Olalo, Pilar, Capiz

In one of the mountains of of Olalo, Pilar, Capiz is a humble refuge for tourists seeking a natural experience. The Kataw Falls, born of the ruins of the Azure Mining in the late 70’s, cascades from Kataw Gamay Falls in a higher part of the mountain into a shaded pool and a river.

The name Kataw originated from a folktale known from a long time ago. It was told that during the full moon, the local residents would hear a voice of a woman singing from the middle of the waterfall. In the eagerness to find out who the maiden was, they sneaked to the mountains and found out that it was a mermaid. In awe, they exclaimed, “Kataw! Kataw!” which is the native term for mermaid. Thus, the place was named.

Suhot Cave and Spring

Dolores, Dumalag, Capiz

Located just 300 meters away from the provincial road, the Suhot Cave and Spring in Dumalag, Capiz is a series of interconnected caverns of different sizes with a pool of clear ice-cold water fed by a rock spring from within the cave. Suhot is believed to have a connecting tunnel to the Badiang Cave in Dumarao since both caves are found in the same mountain and are only 6 kilometers apart.

Gugma Beach

Lonoy, Sapian, Capiz

The beach is located at the eastern part of Lonoy. It is rich when it comes to different water resources such as fishes and aquatic resources. This beach in Sapian is praised because of its rock formations, sea breeze and bluish water that attract tourists.
