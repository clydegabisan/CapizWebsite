EVENTS AND FESTIVALS

CAPIZTAHAN FESTIVAL

Province of Capiz

Capiztahan is a celebration of the birth of the Province of Capiz. On April 15, 1901, the United States Philippines Commission through Act. No. 115, entitled, “An Act Extending The Provisions Of The Provincial Government Act To The Province Of Capiz” established the province which now have one (1) city and sixteen (16) municipalities, divided into two districts.

Capiztahan is a celebration filled with numerous colorful activities and showcases not only the abundance of marine life and gastronomic cuisines, but also the unique interplay of shared history and diverse culture in the way of its people’s lives. This grand festival is not only to commemorate the foundation of the province but also the death anniversary of the late President Manuel A. Roxas.

SINADYA SA INMACULADA CONCEPCION

Roxas City

Sinadya literally means joy. The festival is held as a thanksgiving event for the city’s bounties especially those from the seas. The festival is a week-long celebration during the first week of December also commemorating the feast of the Immaculate Concepcion.

PATABANG Festival

Municipality of Tapaz

Patabang was conceived to give identity to the Tapaznon people’s generosity of heart and the idea of coming into each other for help which is reflected in their way of life. “Pagpatabang” is one valuable trait of Tapaznons shown especially in times of great difficulty. It is also manifested during the planting and harvesting season, in preparing food during thanksgiving, in moving transferring a hut from one place to another which is a very iconic display of the bayanhihan spirit, and in many more activities in the Tapaznon’s simple way of living.

SINA-OT Festival

Municipality of President Roxas

Sinaot Festival was inspired by the Masskara Festival of Bacolod City that started in 1988, it is a street dancing contest among groups dancing to the tune of the Latin beat garbed in colorful Caribbean costume, depicting the culture and traditions of each tribe. This annual festival is held every 1st week of May, is the center piece of the town’s municipal and religious fiesta.

SAULOG Festival

Municipality of President Roxas

Saulog Festival commemorates the Foundation day of the municipality of President Roxas. It is celebrated every 30th day of November each year. President Roxas was originally the small community of Lutod-lutod, Barrio Aranguel in the old town of Pilar.  

The municipality was named in honor of President Manuel A. Roxas, the First President of the Third Republic of the Philippines, and was officially inaugurated on November 30, 1949 under Republic Act No. 374 that was signed into law by Elpidio Quirino.

Saulog is festive celebration that begins with an early morning drumbeat playing or “pamukaw-pukaw.” Other activities include Drum and Lyre Exhibition, civic parade, Battle of the Bands, Food Festival, Battle of the Sounds, Foundation Ball and the Lin-ay sang President Roxas Pageant.

HIL-O HIL-O Festival

Municipality of Maayon

Hil-o Hil-o Festival connotes the exchange of mutual interdependence among the people of Maayon which can be expressed in many forms. It could simply be the act of offering a helping hand, rendering of services and giving of material donations for a relative, a friend or a neighbor who is holding a very important affair in the family. This Festival is celebrated annually every 3rd week of February for three (3) days starting Thursday.

GUYUM – GUYUMAN Festival

Municipality of Pontevedra

Guyum-Guyum Festival came from “Caguyuman,” the old name of Pontevedra, which means an ant hill. Because of its rich and abundant natural resources both from the coastal and upland areas, it attracted people from the nearby municipalities of Panay to which it once belonged. People from Panitan, Maayon and President Roxas would thrive in the market place of Pontevedra to trade, like a swarm of ants. Thus, the birth of Caguyuman.  The festival is the tradition in celebrating life and as well as a way thanksgiving for the abundance that the Caguyumanons was blessed.

INILUSAN Festival

Municipality of Mambusao

Inilusan was first introduced in 1975 in celebration of Mambusao’s annual cultural–religious festivity. This is a colourful pageant celebrated by way of recollecting the stories of Mambusao and depicting the historical and cultural growth of the town during the celebration of the town fiesta on November 25 in honour of Saint Catherine of Alexandria. The term Inilusan means the mutual and reciprocal giving and sharing of food and resources in generous measure and in the spirit of fraternal goodwill that permeates the daily life of Mambusaonons.  Inilusan is celebrated with novenas and masses, merry-making, cultural shows, street dancing, parades, exhibits, a beauty pageant, food festival.

TUGBONG Festival

Municipality of Panitan

Tugbong Festival is a celebration filled with merry making, dancing, singing street dancing parade and many others joyous activities to give honor and recognition to every Paniteño who have contributed to the social, cultural, educational and economic development of the town and also to pay homage to their “kasimanwa” whose successes in life gave inspiration to the young generations.  

Tugbong is also a celebration of the local customs, traditions, religious, and political activities during August 1-10 of every year. Before the highlight of this celebration in honor of St. Lawrence the Deacon, people from far barangay usually go down to town to buy their goods and other food to serve in their banquet for sharing and consumption. Hence, the practice is called tugbong that reflect the old ways of the Paniteños when they go down from the mountainous part of the town to visit their relatives and to trade. They carry all the way down livestock and agricultural products in exchange for any goods that would sustain their living. Historians called this “alista” or “tugbong.”

HIL-O-HANAY Festival

Municipality of Sigma

Hil-o Hanay Festival is a celebration of the Sigmahanon virtues of sharing, giving, volunteerism and oneness. It is also the way of showing this rich cultural tradition to the younger generation to continue the Hil-o-hanay spirit with passion. It is an attitude that denotes the act of voluntarily returning a favor done by another person. It gives honor to the noblest and highest virtue of “community-ness” of its people, their innate and common traits that keep the balance of their relationship with one another, their environment, and God.

Activities during this festive occasion include Payag Contest, Pailig it Lantay, Pagbunggad, Lutong Sigmahanon, Traditional Games, Composo, Harana, Duag Hil-o-hanya and Hil-ohanay sa Plaza. It is also in celebration of the Feast of St. John the Baptist.

AGDAHANAY Festival

Municipality of Cuartero

Agadahanay Festival is celebrated every second week of June in honor of San Antonio de Padua, the patron saint of the town. "Agdahanay," is coined based on the people’s generosity of heart as reflected in their helpfulness, hospitality, sharing of concern for others and the “pagpang-agda”. The Agdahanay spirit lives on every Cuarteronhon in abundance or insufficiency and especially during disasters and calamities.

One of the highlights of the Agdahanay Festival is the procession of the image of San Antonio de Padua along the major streets with another caro that collects food or “Pandecillo de San Antonio” as the people’s way to offer thanksgiving to the Lord for the peace that reigns in the town and the blessings that they received through the intercession of San Antonio de Padua. It is followed by a High Mass and the Sayaw kay San Antonio de Padua, a dance ritual performed by young girls each holding a rope tied to the top of the pole. The fast-paced dancing while weaving the rope around the pole is the most anticipated part of this dance. Only after that when all Cuarteronhon’s open their houses’ doors for their guests.

LINGA-ANAY FESTIVAL

Municipality of Panay

Lingga-anay Festival, a vivid and accurate interpretation of the Pan-ayanons’ deep love for our birthplace, strong sense of responsibility toward our fellow Panayanons is held every May 1-4 annually. The term “lingga-anay” was derived from the Big Bell or “Dakung Lingganay” of the Sta. Monica Church known to be the Biggest Catholic Church Bell in Southeast Asia which the town of Panay is well-known for, not only locally but around the world. But on a patriotic note, “lingga-anay” means “to look back,” conveying to the Pan-ayanons outside the country to look back, come home and encourage them to invest on their homeland. The festival began in 1987 during the Appointment to office of Vicente Bermejo.

PADAGYAW Festival

Municipality of Dumarao

Padagyaw Festival mirrors the unity and cooperation of its people, and is celebrated every first week of August in honor of its patroness Nuestra Señora de las Nieves (Our Lady of Snows). Festival activities like the Pista ng Bayan, Civic Parade, Street Dancing Competition, Playground Demonstration and more manifests how Dumaraonon value group effort. From one fermented idea of planners, action men, and executions, they have translated their ideas and concepts into mass action of well-rehearsed and well-executed play and graceful rendition of songs and dances.  

Padagyaw is a term of the natives of the town that mean the need for voluntary help in order to accomplish a task, without having to pay cash for the service rendered. As a thanksgiving, they gather for feast and merry making with the understanding that everyone partakes from the success of one. Padagyaw is a commonly used term by everyone especially by the Dumaraonon elders and is recognized as an indispensable tradition that has been passed from generation to generation.

TILIBYUGAN Festival

Municipality of Sapian

The Tilibyugan Festival is the merging of two Festivals of Sapian celebrated from every 22nd to the 26th day of July. This festival is the grandest fiesta in honor of Saint Anne, their patron, during her month-feast, July of each year. Originating from the vernacular word “tibyug” which means to lift up or to support, tilibyugay of the Sapianons subscribes to the concept of lifting each other and supporting one another for a common welfare. Consequently, the said festival enhances their appreciation of cultural heritage.
