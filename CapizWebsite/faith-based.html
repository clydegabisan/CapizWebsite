FAITH-BASED

Sacred Heart of Jesus Christ Shrine

Dinginan, Roxas City, Capiz

Rising 132 feet (40 m) from the ground, this giant statute of the Sacred Heart of Jesus is considered as the largest life-like statue of Jesus Christ in the Philippines and is located in Pueblo de Panay, Dinginan, Roxas City, Capiz. Overlooking the scenic view of the city, the Sacred Heart of Jesus Shrine has become a pilgrimage site and a perfect place for meditation and peace.

Its construction lasted for almost four years (2011-2015) and was done by a seven-man team lead by a Filipino sculptor John Alaban, and the foreman, Jose Clarito. The Sacred Heart of Jesus is the patron of the Ong family. The statue was erected as a gift from a cousin to one of the Ong siblings who wished to build a sculpture of reverence on a hilltop that is facing the sea.

Agtalin Shrine

Pilar, Capiz

Standing 80 feet from the top of a hill is the erected image of the Lady of the Miraculous Medal that draws thousands of devotees every first Saturday of the month to attend mass. Known as the Agtalin Shrine, it was built through the efforts of the former mayor of the municipality and the Agtalin Development Foundation.

The idea came of the mayor who suffered through a serious stroke due to Coronary Thrombosis or the formation of a blood clot inside a blood vessel of the heart. When he was brought to Pilar Medicare and Community Hospital, he was almost unconscious when he saw the Blessed Virgin with something round and black at her back descending from the top of the hill to the highway. When the Blessed Virgin reached the street, he regained consciousness.

When he was transferred to St. Anthony Hospital in Roxas City, he saw the image of the Lady of the Miraculous Medal on the calendar hanging in his room that looked exactly as the Blessed Virgin he saw in his vision, and have told the Mother Superior of the hospital of his plan to construct the Marian image on top of Agtalin Hill.

The shrine was erected in 1993, on top of the Agtalin Hill in Dulangan, Pilar, Capiz, and has since then became a pilgrimage site for the pilgrims of the Lady of the Miraculous Medal.

Our Lady of Lourdes Grotto Meditation Hills

Maninang, Sapian, Capiz

Nestled on top of a hill among giant boulders that seem landscaped by a divine force in the middle of a wide area of well-manicured carabao grass surrounded by tall trees, this grotto is a place of beauty and peace – a perfect location for meditation and prayer.  Climbing the hill with a fresh escort of nature; cool breeze, clear water in a creek seeping out from a rock and various species of birds, to the life-size statues of Our Lady of Lourdes and St. Bernadette is quite fulfilling. A chapel rests on the bottom of the hill where Saturday masses are held. A sanctuary of peace and serenity, the idea of the meditation hills came from the dream of Jose Villanueva. He dreamed that Mary was standing on a rock that is located in that place where Mary’s image is now standing.
